The Great Gatsby

Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald

A tragic story of Jay Gatsby and his unrequited love for Daisy Buchanan set in the Roaring Twenties. Gatsby's wealth and lavish parties mask his lonely and obsessive pursuit of a past romance, highlighting themes of ambition, excess, and the American Dream's elusive nature.

To Kill a Mockingbird

Author:Harper Lee

The story of Atticus Finch, a lawyer in the Depression-era South, and his defense of a black man unjustly accused of rape. Through the eyes of his young daughter Scout, the novel explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and the loss of innocence in a prejudiced society.

Dune

Author:Frank Herbert

An epic science fiction novel about the young Paul Atreides and his journey on the desert planet Arrakis. Amidst political intrigue and interstellar conflict, Paul discovers his destiny and navigates a world of prophecy, ecological transformation, and the struggle for a powerful substance known as "spice."

Pride and Prejudice

Author:Jane Austen

A classic romance novel that explores the themes of love and societal expectations in 19th century England. The story follows the spirited Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates issues of class, marriage, and morality, particularly in her interactions with the enigmatic Mr. Darcy.
